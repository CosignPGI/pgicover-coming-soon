What is Personal Guarantee Insurance?

Personal Guarantee Insurance (PGI) protects business owners who are required to sign personal guarantees on business loans. If your business defaults and the lender enforces the guarantee, PGI covers a substantial portion of your personal obligation—protecting your home, savings, and personal assets.

When you sign a personal guarantee, you're putting your personal wealth on the line. PGI transforms this open-ended risk into a known, manageable insurance premium. You get the capital you need to grow, while protecting what you've worked a lifetime to build.

Common Questions

You purchase a PGI policy when taking on a business loan that requires a personal guarantee. If your business later defaults and the lender enforces the guarantee against you personally, PGI pays a covered portion of the debt—reducing what you owe out of pocket.
PGI covers personal guarantees on most business financing, including SBA loans, term loans, lines of credit, equipment financing, real estate loans, and acquisition financing. If you're signing a personal guarantee, there's likely a PGI solution available.
Coverage amounts depend on the loan size, your business's financial health, and underwriting criteria. You retain some exposure to maintain alignment with the lender, but the majority of your risk is transferred to the insurance policy. Contact us for a quote based on your specific situation.
The best time is before or when you sign the personal guarantee. However, PGI can also cover existing guarantees. Whether you're refinancing, acquiring a business, or expanding operations, PGI can be structured to fit your timeline.

More Questions

PGI is a contract between you and the insurer—not the lender. Your personal guarantee with the lender remains in full force. The insurance simply provides you with coverage if that guarantee is enforced. Most lenders view PGI favorably as it demonstrates sophisticated risk management.
A claim is typically triggered when your business defaults on the loan, the lender calls in your personal guarantee, and you face a demand to pay personally. The specific terms vary by policy, but PGI is designed to pay when your personal assets are genuinely at risk.
Premium varies based on the guarantee amount, loan type, your business's financial health, and your credit profile. When compared to the potential cost of losing your home or life savings, most business owners find PGI provides meaningful value. Get a quote to see your specific cost.
